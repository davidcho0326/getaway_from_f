## 1. "Why do you want to work at Anthropic?" (Required, 200-400 words)

Your JD says "educate them about LLMs" and separately says "design innovative sales strategies." I've spent the past year discovering that these are the same activity.

I've trained startup C-level executives across 6 industries -- from fashion to automotive R&D to K-pop entertainment -- on Claude-based AI integration. Not as a side project, but as a deliberate sales methodology: education eliminates the psychological resistance that cold outreach never can. By session 3, skeptics become technical integration planners. One engagement converted directly into a commercial contract. The demo was the deployment; the classroom was the sales floor.

But I didn't arrive at this strategy by reading a playbook. I arrived by failing -- specifically, by trying four different approaches to tag 150,000 fashion images and honestly abandoning three of them before Claude VLM API solved it in five days at 90% accuracy. That willingness to abandon what doesn't work and rebuild from first principles isn't just a problem-solving habit. It's the core of how I sell: I ask questions before I pitch, I build trust before I close, and I design constraints before I ship features.

That last point is what draws me to Anthropic specifically. When I built production MCP servers (50+ tools, 12-criterion quality validation, 11 auto-fail gates), the hardest decisions were about what AI shouldn't do -- preventing anatomically incorrect images from reaching marketing teams, ensuring brand safety in automated pipelines. I spent more time on guardrails than features. Then I realized: that's exactly what Anthropic does at scale. The alignment between how I build and how Anthropic thinks felt less like a career choice and more like recognition.

Over eight years, I've driven KRW 3-4B in annual deal flow with 85% client retention, embedded AI into the workflows of 320 employees across two countries, and scaled an AI platform from KRW 40M to 1B. I'm not approaching this role as someone who wants to learn about Claude. I've built production systems on it. I want to bring the education-as-sales methodology I've developed to the one company whose product I already believe in -- and whose approach to safety mirrors my own.
