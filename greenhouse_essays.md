## 1. "Why do you want to work at Anthropic?" (Required, 200-400 words)

In December 2023, I had a problem no vendor could solve cost-effectively: tagging 150,000 fashion images with consistent taxonomy across 47 categories. I'm not a developer — I studied Philosophy. But Claude didn't just help me write the code; it helped me think through the problem from first principles. Five days later, the system was running at 90% accuracy with 90% cost reduction. That week changed my career.

But what kept me thinking about Anthropic wasn't the capability — it was the constraint. When I later built production MCP servers for my company (17 tools, 12-criterion quality validation, 11 auto-fail gates), the hardest design decisions weren't about what AI could do, but what it shouldn't. How do you prevent anatomically incorrect images from reaching marketing teams? How do you ensure brand safety in an automated pipeline? I found myself spending more time on guardrails than on features — and then I realized that's exactly what Anthropic does at a civilizational scale. That alignment felt less like a career choice and more like recognition.

Over the past two years, I've led AI adoption for 320 employees across Korea and China, reported AI strategy directly to our CEO, and built three production MCP servers with 50+ tools. But the work that convinced me to apply was something I started doing on my own: training startup C-level executives on Claude-based AI integration. Watching a CEO go from "how do I prompt this?" to "how do I connect MCP to our data pipeline?" in five sessions taught me something — the hardest barrier to AI adoption isn't technical. It's trust. Founders don't need another capabilities pitch. They need someone who has lived the journey: the failed vendors, the organizational resistance, and ultimately the transformation.

That's what draws me to this role. I'm not approaching it as someone who wants to learn about Claude. I've built production systems on it. I'm approaching it as someone who has already walked the path that startup customers will walk — and wants to guide them through it with the one company that takes safety as seriously as capability.
